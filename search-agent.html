SearchAgent: Optimizing Commercial Due Diligence through Brief-Aware AI

700+ associates were spending hours scrolling raw search results to find the right experts for every commercial due diligence project. I redesigned SearchAgent — the internal tool that powers expert discovery — evolving it from a manual database filter into a brief-aware, AI-ranked discovery engine.

V1 established the infrastructure. V2 made it intelligent.

Strategic Challenge

From Data Filterer to Project Strategist

SearchAgent V2 was a planned evolution, not a failure-driven redesign. V1 centralised fragmented discovery into a single database — but it remained a high-effort search tool requiring associates to manually scan hundreds of raw results. V2 introduced a semantic layer that interprets the project brief, moving users from manual retrieval to predictive discovery.

⚠
The Core Problem

The bottleneck wasn't scope — it was signal. Associates were drowning in unranked results.

The Process

V2 didn't start with a blank page. I ran V1 stabilisation and V2 exploration in parallel.

Phase 1 — Stabilise & Explore

Months 1–6: Stabilise V1 while defining SearchAgent's future

When I became UX lead for the Automation stream, V1 was already in active use. I focused on stabilizing it through quick wins — heuristic improvements, better filtering, and early customer exploration — while building the case for a more fundamental V2 redesign. It was a deliberate bridging strategy: keep V1 usable and trustworthy while building the evidence base to justify a V2 investment.

In parallel, I worked with Product, Data Science, and Engineering to define the future direction. We explored multi-perspective views and bulk workflows, ultimately focusing on Competitors and Customers to cover the highest-impact use cases while keeping V2 feasible to deliver.

Insight

Early exploration revealed that ranking — not scope — was the core friction. Associates didn't need more data; they needed better signal.

Phase 2 — Evidence-Led Convergence

Research study with 15 global users — MBB account holders and project managers

To validate V2 direction, I led a global research study across MBB accounts. Through technical negotiation with Engineering, we identified that Confirmed Customer Lists would delay launch by months — and research confirmed it wasn't a top user need. I steered the design toward a Suggested list within each competitor company — an AI-ranked view of the most relevant experts per brief — while keeping confirmed customers visible within each competitor view as in V1. The same pattern later extended to Customer companies.

"

70% of MBB account holders said a "ready-to-use shortlist" would be a game-changer for speed.

Finding

Users define quality by expert recency (last 12 months) and previous screening by colleagues.

"I would expect to see experts screened in previous projects... recently screened, e.g., the last 6 months."

Design Decision

Ranking logic surfaces highest-incentive and most-consulted experts first. For customer expert profiles specifically, ranking prioritises product/service familiarity — current users ranked first — and quote recency.

Finding

80% of users are company-agnostic — they need any customer of a competitor. Only 20% require deep dives into specific entities.

"In 80% of cases, the client doesn't care for the specific customer companies, they just need customer profiles of X competitors."

Design Decision

Maintained a consolidated Customer View under each competitor while enabling drill-downs for targeted recruitment.

Finding

PMs prioritise a consolidated ready-to-send list for speed. Associates require company-specific lists for team delegation.

"This is a game changer... Having for example a 15-people ready-to-send FL would be great."

Design Decision

The Suggested tab re-ranks the expert pool by brief match, providing a curated starting point. The full ready-to-send shortlist is planned for V3.

Finding

Associates leave the platform to use Gemini or Google for company discovery, fragmenting the workflow and losing context.

"I use Gemini to ask for a common ground... and then ask for an expanded list..."

Design Decision

Descoped 'Expand your research' from V2 and flagged it for dedicated research — rather than shipping an untested feature, a deliberate decision to treat scoping discipline as a design outcome.

To validate the information architecture, I tested a single validated concept with the 15 users: a consolidated "Confirmed Customer Experts" view, accessible from each competitor's list and filterable by competitor. Users accessed it pre-filtered from a competitor's entry point, but could adjust the dropdown to see all confirmed customers across the project.

Phase 3 — Operational Migration & V2 Launch

Zero-downtime transition for 700+ associates across four progressive releases

Shipping V2 as a single release would have disrupted 700+ active users. I designed a phased rollout — structural changes first, then AI features, then scope expansion to Customers. Each release was independently stable, giving associates time to build trust in new patterns before the next layer arrived, and creating feedback loops to catch issues early.

To protect the timeline, we scoped strategically: kept the existing modal-based filters instead of migrating to a new drawer pattern, and launched Customer Companies with non-network experts only — covering ~80% of use cases — deferring full integration to V3.

This phased structure also laid the architectural foundation for V3 — evolving SearchAgent from a discovery tool into a project strategist.

Final Solution

I redesigned SearchAgent's core architecture to eliminate context-switching — bringing customer and competitor research into a single, AI-enhanced workflow.

How it Works

The redesigned workflow guides associates from AI-ranked discovery to building their expert pipeline:

  1. 1
    Suggested list

    Associates land on an AI-ranked list of experts per company, ordered by relevance to the project brief. Transparency labels explain why each expert ranks where they do — recency, incentive status, screening history — giving associates confidence in the ranking without removing their control.

  2. 2
    Index to Leads

    Associates use the '+' action to add selected experts into the Leads tab — their working pipeline for the project. From there they track outreach and build toward the first expert list for the client.

  3. 3
    Customer Companies

    A dedicated section surfaces non-network experts from target customer firms, enabling associates to explore a second perspective and broaden their search.

Key Design Decisions

Five decisions shaped the V2 experience — each grounded in research findings and technical negotiation.

  1. 1
    AI-driven re-ranking Core Innovation

    Used LLMs to interpret briefs and re-rank expert pools by project relevance. The Suggested tab offers a curated starting point ahead of full automation in V3. Rankings incorporate signals such as seniority, tenure, recency, incentive, expert status (prior engagement and recency), and customer familiarity from expert quotes, defined with Data Science.

  2. 2
    Integrated customer research

    Built a direct path to find employees at target firms inside SearchAgent, bypassing LinkedIn Sales Navigator entirely. Confirmed customer experts remain accessible within each competitor view, consistent with V1 — no regression in the existing workflow.

  3. 3
    NNSP-first strategy

    Prioritised NNSP (non-network expert) discovery for target customer companies, covering ~80% of use cases. This allowed us to deliver immediate value while deferring the more complex Confirmed Lists infrastructure.

  4. 4
    Explainable AI — transparency as trust Support Tickets: 10% → 2%

    Designed transparency disclaimers and tooltips explaining how each expert was ranked — not just that AI ranked them. This was non-negotiable for adoption: associates needed to understand the logic before trusting the output. Support tickets dropped from 10% to 2%.

  5. 5
    Consolidated Suggested tab

    Surfacing a brief-matched ranked expert list per company, giving PMs an immediate starting point for their first client list. The full ready-to-send shortlist is planned for V3.

Reflections

Trust was the real design challenge. The hardest part wasn't the interface — it was shifting associates from manual, fully controlled workflows to AI-assisted ones. The Suggested tab only worked once users understood why experts were ranked. Transparency wasn't a feature; it was core to adoption.

Research made the tradeoffs defensible. Descoping Confirmed Customer Lists required convincing stakeholders that partial delivery at Q1 was more valuable than a complete solution at Q3. The research gave me the data to make that argument — building the evidence base early, before any design work, was what made the tradeoff defensible.

Impact beyond SearchAgent. The transparency patterns introduced here — ranking disclaimers, explainability tooltips, AI confidence signals — became the design language for how Dialectica's products communicate AI decisions to users. What started as a trust solution for one tool set the standard across the product.

Simplicity is Earned

Initial concepts explored multiple perspectives, but testing with 15 users showed the need to simplify to a single, focused entry point.

Trust Drives Adoption

AI rankings are only useful when users understand them. Collaborating with Data Science on human-readable ranking labels was the decision that unlocked adoption — associates didn't need perfect accuracy, they needed to see why.
